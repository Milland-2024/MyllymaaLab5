Random Space Facts!

Earth is the densest planet while Saturn is the least dense.

The Moon is drifting away from Earth by 3.78 centimetres every year.

Venus' day (rotation), is longer than its revolution (year).

Ganymede, Jupiter's largest moon, has its own magnetic field.

Because of extreme temperature variations, Mercury is technically "shrinking".

The Sun accounts for a little over 99% of the Solar System's mass.

Haumea, a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, looks like an egg with a ring.

The winds on Neptune can go faster than sound.

Uranus was originally going to be named after King George III.

It's commonly thought that Saturn's rings were made from the remnants of a broken moon.

The first animals in space were fruit flies.

Earth is the only planet not named after a god.

Triton, the largest moon of Neptune, revolves around the planet in the opposite direction to the other moons.

Mercury and Venus are the only planets without moons.

A long time ago, the Sun and the Moon were considered planets.

Space is said to smell metallic.
